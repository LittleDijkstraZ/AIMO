Suppose there is a group of 20 people and we know that everyone in the group knows each other. We want to form 5 different groups of 4 people each from this group of 20 people. How many ways can this be done?

Let's denote the number of ways to form 5 groups of 4 people each from a group of 20 people as $N$.

To solve this problem, we can use the concept of combinations. The number of ways to choose 4 people out of 20 is $\binom{20}{4}$, which is the number of combinations of 20 things taken 4 at a time.

After choosing the first group of 4 people, we are left with 16 people. The number of ways to choose the second group of 4 people out of the remaining 16 is $\binom{16}{4}$.

Continuing this process, we find the number of ways to choose the third, fourth, and fifth groups of 4 people to be $\binom{12}{4}$, $\binom{8}{4}$, and $\binom{4}{4}$, respectively.

To find the total number of ways to form the 5 groups, we multiply these combinations together:
$$N = \binom{20}{4} \cdot \binom{16}{4} \cdot \binom{12}{4} \cdot \binom{8}{4} \cdot \binom{4}{4}.$$

We can calculate the individual combinations:
$$\binom{20}{4} = \frac{20!}{4!(20-4)!} = \frac{20!}{4!16!} = 4845,$$
$$\binom{16}{4} = \frac{16!}{4!(16-4)!} = \frac{16!}{4!12!} = 1820,$$
$$\binom{12}{4} = \frac{12!}{4!(12-4)!} = \frac{12!}{4!8!} = 495,$$
$$\binom{8}{4} = \frac{8!}{4!(8-4)!} = \frac{8!}{4!4!} = 70,$$
$$\binom{4}{4} = \frac{4!}{4!(4-4)!} = \frac{4!}{4!0!} = 1.$$

Multiplying these together:
$$N = 4845 \cdot 1820 \cdot 495 \cdot 70 \cdot 1 = 12,487,376,680,000.$$

Therefore, there are 12,487,376,680,000 ways to form 5 different groups of 4 people each from a group of 20 people where everyone knows each other.
The answer is: $$12,487,376,680,000$$

In a box, there are 3 red balls, 4 blue balls, and 7 white balls. If we randomly pick 2 balls out of the box, what is the probability that at least one of the balls we pick is white?

To solve this problem, we can first find the probability of the complementary event (i.e., the probability that neither of the balls we pick is white) and then subtract it from 1 to find the probability that at least one of the balls we pick is white.

The total number of balls in the box is $3 + 4 + 7 = 14$. The number of ways to pick 2 balls out of 14 is $\binom{14}{2} = \frac{14!}{2!12!} = 91$.

The number of ways to pick 2 balls that are not white is the number of ways to pick 2 balls out of the 10 non-white balls ($3 + 4 = 7$). This is $\binom{10}{2} = \frac{10!}{2!8!} = 45$.

So the probability that neither of the balls we pick is white is $\frac{45}{91}$.

The probability that at least one of the balls we pick is white is then $1 - \frac{45}{91} = \frac{46}{91}$.
The answer is: $\frac{46}{91}$

Compute the volume of the solid generated by revolving the region bounded by the curve $y = e^x$, the x-axis, and the lines $x = 0$ and $x = 1$ about the x-axis.

The volume of the solid generated by revolving the region bounded by the curve $y = e^x$, the x-axis, and the lines $x = 0$ and $x = 1$ about the x-axis is given by the disk method. The formula for the volume of a solid of revolution using the disk method is:

$$V = \pi \int_a^b [f(x)]^2 dx$$

In this case, $f(x) = e^x$, $a = 0$, and $b = 1$. So we have:

$$V = \pi \int_0^1 (e^x)^2 dx = \pi \int_0^1 e^{2x} dx$$

To find the integral, we can use substitution. Let $u = 2x$, so $du = 2 dx$. Then, the integral becomes:

$$V = \pi \int_0^2 \frac{1}{2} e^u du$$

Now, we can integrate:

$$V = \pi \left[\frac{1}{2} e^u \right]_0^2 = \pi \left(\frac{1}{2} e^2 - \frac{1}{2} e^0\right) = \pi \left(\frac{1}{2} e^2 - \frac{1}{2}\right)$$

Simplifying, we get:

$$V = \frac{\pi}{2} (e^2 - 1)$$

So the volume of the solid generated by revolving the region bounded by the curve $y = e^x$, the x-axis, and the lines $x = 0$ and $x = 1$ about the x-axis is $\frac{\pi}{2} (e^2 - 1)$.
The answer is: $$\frac{\pi}{2}(e^2-1)$$
